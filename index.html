Research Direction

My research interest lies at the intersection of AI, cultural technology, human-centered design, and computational social science. I am particularly interested in how recommendation algorithms, digital platforms, and generative AI systems influence cultural discovery, taste formation, emotional experience, and user decision-making.

Drawing on my background in sociology and applied data science, I aim to study not only whether AI systems perform well technically, but also how they reshape the way people encounter films, music, media content, services, and cultural meaning.

How can AI systems recommend not only what users are likely to click, but what may become meaningful in their cultural and emotional lives?

AI-Based SaaS Churn Prediction & Retention Strategy

Machine Learning · XAI · Streamlit

An explainable AI project for predicting customer churn and translating model outputs into actionable retention strategies.

  • Built an account-level churn prediction pipeline using subscription, feature usage, support ticket, and account data.
  • Excluded churn event records from model features to prevent data leakage and used them for post-hoc interpretation.
  • Engineered behavioral and operational features such as active subscription ratio, error rate, support response time, days since last usage, and recent upgrade/downgrade flags.
  • Compared machine learning and deep learning models, tuned the operational threshold, and used SHAP for global and local explanation.
  • Developed a Streamlit dashboard to communicate churn risk, model reasoning, and retention actions.

Master’s Thesis: Life Satisfaction among Middle-Aged and Older Retirees

Quantitative Sociology · Panel Data · Stata

A sociological study using Korean panel data to examine factors associated with life satisfaction among retirees.

  • Used KLoSA panel data to analyze the life satisfaction of middle-aged and older retirees.
  • Examined both objective and subjective predictors, including socioeconomic status, perceived social class, and self-rated health.
  • Found that subjective indicators explained life satisfaction more strongly than many objective measures.
  • Developed a long-term research interest in how subjective experience can be operationalized and analyzed through data.

Why Cultural Technology?

My intellectual path has been shaped by both cultural curiosity and analytical training. Since adolescence, I have been deeply engaged with films and music, not only as entertainment but as cultural experiences that shape emotion, memory, identity, and social imagination.

Through sociology, I learned to ask how individuals are shaped by structures, institutions, and cultural contexts. Through data science and AI projects, I learned how large-scale behavioral traces can be modeled, interpreted, and translated into decisions.

Cultural Technology is the field where these paths converge. I hope to study AI not merely as a technical system, but as a cultural infrastructure that increasingly mediates what people watch, listen to, remember, desire, and value.
